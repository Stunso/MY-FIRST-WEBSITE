THE SUPRASPINATUS MUSCLE

The supraspinatus is one of the four rotator cuff muscles that stabilize the shoulder joint and initiate arm abduction. Originating in the supraspinous fossa of the scapula, it runs under the acromion to insert on the greater tubercle of the humerus. The supraspinatus is crucial for shoulder stability and movement but is also the most frequently injured rotator cuff muscle, leading to conditions like supraspinatus tendinitis and tears. Anatomy Origin: The supraspinatus muscle originates in the supraspinous fossa, a depression above the spine of the scapula. Insertion: It then extends laterally, passes under the acromion process, and inserts onto the superior facet of the greater tubercle of the humerus. Rotator Cuff: The supraspinatus is one of the four muscles that make up the rotator cuff, along with the infraspinatus, teres minor, and subscapularis. Functions Arm Abduction: Its primary action is the initial abduction (lifting) of the arm at the shoulder joint. Shoulder Stabilization: Along with the other rotator cuff muscles, it helps to stabilize the head of the humerus in the glenoid fossa, providing dynamic stability to the shoulder joint.
